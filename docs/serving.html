🙌

What Serving Means

For Adam, serving is not a program. It's not a box to check or a role on a church org chart. It's a posture — a way of moving through the world where every platform, every resource, and every relationship becomes an opportunity to give, not take.

The Marine Corps taught Adam to serve the mission above himself. Christ took that even further — calling His followers to serve even those who have nothing to offer in return. Bow & Arrow, Noble Warriors, the local church, the marketplace — all of it becomes Kingdom territory when the heart is oriented toward service.

How You Can Serve

You don't need a title to serve. You need availability.

The greatest barrier to service isn't lack of skills, resources, or opportunity. It's the belief that you need to be further along before you can give something away. That belief is a lie.

  • Serve where you are — in your home, your workplace, your neighborhood
  • Serve with what you have — time, a truck, a meal, a listening ear
  • Serve who is in front of you — stop waiting for the "right" person
  • Get planted in a local church — structured service builds character

"You don't need a title to serve. You need availability."
