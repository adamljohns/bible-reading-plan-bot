My Story

I'm Adam Johns — most people call me Moop. After 20 years of military service in the United States Marine Corps, I walked out of uniform with a conviction: discipline is a gift, not a punishment. The Corps taught me what the human body is capable of under pressure, under load, under fire. That never left me.

I became a NASM-certified trainer because I wanted to bring that same standard to everyday people — not elite athletes, not gym rats — regular people who want to feel strong, live long, and show up fully for their families. I specialize in body composition, visceral fat reduction, and the behavioral side of sustainable change. Because the hardest part of fitness isn't the workout. It's the identity shift underneath it.

At fit20 Cosner's Corner, I've seen what 20 minutes of properly programmed slow-rep resistance training does to people who thought they'd never have time to train. It changes their posture. Their confidence. Their relationship with their own body. That's the work I was made for.

What to Expect Working With Me

I don't do cookie-cutter programs. Every client starts with a full intake assessment — your movement history, body composition goals, nutritional habits, sleep, recovery, and behavioral readiness. The NASM CNC framework I use puts your psychology ahead of your program, because a plan you won't follow isn't a plan.

Whether we train together at fit20 or I'm coaching you remotely, you'll get:

  • A personalized training approach built around the fit20 slow-rep HIT method
  • Nutrition guidance anchored to protein targets, visceral fat reduction, and NEAT
  • Biometric tracking: body fat %, HRV, waist circumference — not just the scale
  • Accountability that's direct, honest, and grounded in faith and discipline
  • A coach who's walked it — 20 years military + 2 years in the studio

I specialize in working with men 35–60 who are serious about longevity, body recomposition, and becoming the physical example their families need.
