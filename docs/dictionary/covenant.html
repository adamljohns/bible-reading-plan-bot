Covenant
/ˈkʌv.ə.nənt/
noun / verb
From Old French covenant (agreement), from Latin convenire (to come together, to agree); con- (together) + venire (to come). Hebrew: berith (בְּרִית) — covenant, binding agreement, treaty; possibly from bara (to cut) — covenants were ratified by cutting animals (Gen 15). Greek: diathēkē (διαθήκη) — covenant, testament, will.

📖 Biblical Definition

A covenant in Scripture is a solemn, binding, oath-sworn commitment between parties that creates a relationship with defined obligations and consequences. It is far more than a contract (which is merely transactional) — a covenant binds persons, not just goods. The entire biblical narrative is structured by God's covenants: Noahic (Gen 9), Abrahamic (Gen 12, 15, 17), Mosaic (Exod 19–24), Davidic (2 Sam 7), and the New Covenant (Jer 31:31–34; Luke 22:20). Each covenant has parties, terms, signs, and consequences. The New Covenant, ratified by Christ's blood, fulfills and supersedes the Mosaic covenant — writing the law on hearts rather than stone (Jer 31:33; Heb 8:10). The Bible itself is called the "Old" and "New" Testament — testamentum being the Latin translation of diathēkē.

▶ 📜 Webster 1828 Definition

COVENANT, n. 1. A mutual consent or agreement of two or more persons, to do or to forbear some act or thing; a contract; stipulation. 2. In theology, the covenant of works, is that implied in the commands, prohibitions, and promises of God; the promise of God to man, that obedience should be rewarded with life, and the threatening that disobedience should be punished with death. 3. The covenant of grace, is that by which God engages to give salvation to man, on condition that man believes in Christ and repents of his sins. 4. In church affairs, a solemn agreement between the members of a church, to walk together in brotherly union, according to the rules of the Gospel.

▶ ⚠️ Modern Corruption

Modern culture has replaced covenant with contract — a subtle but devastating shift. A contract is between self-interested parties who maintain mutual benefit as the condition of the relationship; it can be voided when terms aren't met. A covenant is unconditional commitment of person to person regardless of circumstances. This explains the collapse of marriage: when viewed as a contract, it can be dissolved when "not working." Viewed as covenant, it is a binding oath before God that endures through hardship. The therapeutic culture's preference for "healthy boundaries" over covenant commitment has produced a generation that exits relationships at the first sign of cost. God's covenants, notably, are often unilateral — maintained by his faithfulness regardless of human faithlessness (Rom 3:3–4).

📖 Key Scripture

• Genesis 15:17–18 — God passes between the animal pieces alone — a self-maledictory oath, taking the consequences on himself.

• Jeremiah 31:31–34 — The promise of the New Covenant: law written on hearts, sins remembered no more.

• Luke 22:20 — "This cup that is poured out for you is the new covenant in my blood."

• Hebrews 8:6 — "Christ…is the mediator of a better covenant, which is enacted on better promises."

• 2 Samuel 7:12–16 — The Davidic Covenant: an everlasting throne from David's line — fulfilled in Christ.

🔗 Greek & Hebrew Roots

H1285 — berith (בְּרִית): covenant; appears ~287 times in the OT; ratified by cutting animals (Gen 15), by shared meals (Gen 26:30), or by monuments (Gen 31:44–46).

G1242 — diathēkē (διαθήκη): covenant, will, testament; in Greek, this word was primarily used for a "last will" — emphasizing the unconditional, testamentary character of God's promises.
