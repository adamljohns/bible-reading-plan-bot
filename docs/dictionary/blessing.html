Blessing
/ˈblɛs.ɪŋ/
noun
Old English blēdsian (to consecrate with blood); later merged with blīthsian (to make happy). Hebrew: barak (בָּרַךְ), to kneel, to bless — suggesting both receiving from above and bestowing upon another.

Biblical Definition

A blessing in Scripture is the active bestowal of divine favor — God speaking good things into existence over a person, family, or nation. God blesses by His word (Gen. 1:22), by His name (Num. 6:24–26), and through His covenant promises. To bless is not merely to wish well but to impart something real. The Aaronic Blessing (Num. 6) was not a wish — it was a declaration that placed God's name, and thus His presence and power, upon His people. Blessing flows downward from God and is meant to flow outward through His people to the nations (Gen. 12:2–3). All true blessing finds its fullest expression in Christ, "who has blessed us with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places" (Eph. 1:3).

▶ Webster 1828 Definition

BLESSING, n. 1. Benediction; a wish of happiness to another; a solemn or affectionate invocation of happiness. 2. Any means of happiness; a gift, benefit, or advantage. 3. Among the Jews, a present; a gift; as when Jacob said to Esau, "Take my blessing." 4. In Scripture, divine favor; the favor of God. 5. The advantage or benefit conferred by divine favor.

▶ Modern Corruption

Modern culture has secularized "blessing" into a synonym for material prosperity ("I'm so blessed!" over a new car) or mere good luck ("bless up"). Prosperity theology has weaponized it, teaching that faithfulness guarantees financial reward — reducing God's blessing to a divine ATM. Meanwhile, secular culture uses "blessed" sarcastically or as hashtag humility. Scripture's deeper vision — that blessing is primarily relational (God's favorable presence) and purposeful (to make us a blessing to others) — is largely lost in both directions.

Key Scripture

Numbers 6:24–26 — "The LORD bless you and keep you; the LORD make His face shine upon you and be gracious to you; the LORD lift up His countenance upon you and give you peace."

Genesis 12:2–3 — "I will bless you and make your name great, so that you will be a blessing… and in you all the families of the earth shall be blessed."

Ephesians 1:3 — "Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us in Christ with every spiritual blessing in the heavenly places."

Matthew 5:3–11 — The Beatitudes — "Blessed are the poor in spirit, for theirs is the kingdom of heaven…"

Psalm 1:1–2 — "Blessed is the man who walks not in the counsel of the wicked… but his delight is in the law of the LORD."

🔗 Greek & Hebrew Roots

H1288 — barak (בָּרַךְ): to bless, to kneel; conveys the idea of endowing with power for success and prosperity.

G2127 — eulogeō (εὐλογέω): to speak well of, to bless; from eu (well) + logos (word) — blessing as good words spoken with authority.

G3107 — makarios (μακάριος): blessed, happy, fortunate — used in the Beatitudes; describes the state of one under divine favor.
