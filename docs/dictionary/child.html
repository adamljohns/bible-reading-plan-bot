Child
/tʃaɪld/
noun
Old English cild — infant, young person; from Proto-Germanic *kelþam. The plural 'children' preserves an old double-plural form, reflecting how central children were to Old English society.

📖 Biblical Definition

A human being in the early stage of life, created in the image of God, bearing a soul of infinite worth from the moment of conception. Children are a heritage from the Lord and a reward (Psalm 127:3) — not burdens, accidents, or extensions of parental ambition, but sovereign image-bearers entrusted to parents for a season. Scripture calls parents to train, discipline, teach, and love their children, while warning against exasperating them.

📜 Webster 1828 Definition

CHILD, n. A very young person of either sex, generally applied to the period from birth to the age of puberty. In a Scriptural sense, a child of God is one regenerated by the Spirit and adopted into the family of God. To be childlike is to exemplify the humility and trust that Christ commended.

⚠️ Modern Corruption

Children are increasingly viewed as lifestyle choices to be optimized rather than souls to be formed. Abortion denies personhood to children in the womb. The sexual revolution imposes adult sexual ideology on children through school curricula. Gender ideology tells children their bodies are mistakes. Scripture treats children as gifts; modernity treats them as problems to be managed, projects to be improved, or — worst — burdens to be eliminated.
