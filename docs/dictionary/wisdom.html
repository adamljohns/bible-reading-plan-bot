Wisdom
/ˈwɪzdəm/
noun
Old English wīsdōm (knowledge, learning, experience) from wīs (wise) + dōm (judgment, decree). Greek: sophia (σοφία) — wisdom, skill, expertise in living. Hebrew: chokmah (חָכְמָה) — wisdom, skill, the ability to order life rightly according to God's design.

📖 Biblical Definition

Biblical wisdom is the skill of living rightly before God — not mere intelligence or accumulated knowledge, but the practical ability to navigate life according to the way God designed it. "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom" (Prov. 9:10) — all true wisdom is grounded in a right relationship with God and a reverent recognition of His authority. The Hebrew chokmah was used for craftsmen (Ex. 31:3), judges, and kings — skill applied to a task. Wisdom in Scripture applies the same skill to the task of human living. In the NT, Christ Himself is called "the wisdom of God" (1 Cor. 1:24) — the ultimate expression of God's design for human flourishing.

• Proverbs 9:10 — "The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom, and knowledge of the Holy One is understanding."

• James 1:5 — "If any of you lacks wisdom, let him ask God, who gives generously to all without reproach, and it will be given him."

• 1 Corinthians 1:24 — "Christ the power of God and the wisdom of God."

• Proverbs 3:5–7 — "Trust in the LORD with all your heart, and do not lean on your own understanding…Be not wise in your own eyes."

• Colossians 2:3 — In Christ "are hidden all the treasures of wisdom and knowledge."

🔗 Greek & Hebrew Roots

G4678 — sophia (σοφία): wisdom, skill, expertise. In NT, the divine wisdom that surpasses human understanding — and the name for the wisdom of God incarnate in Christ.

G5428 — phronēsis (φρόνησις): practical wisdom, prudence, discernment. The wisdom that makes right judgments in concrete situations.

H2451 — chokmah (חָכְמָה): wisdom, skill, expertise. Used for skilled craftsmen, wise rulers, and God Himself — wisdom as the ability to do the right thing in each situation.

Modern culture has severed wisdom from its root in the fear of God and replaced it with information access, credentials, and expertise. "Wise" now means someone with data — a scientist, statistician, or credentialed professional. Google is the new oracle. But Scripture teaches that a brilliant atheist with a doctoral degree, having rejected the foundational premise of wisdom (the fear of the LORD), is a fool. The modern expert class confuses knowledge with wisdom, technique with understanding, and innovation with progress. Cultural "wisdom" often consists of therapeutic maxims ("live your truth") that directly contradict the ancient wisdom of Scripture on human nature and flourishing.

PIE *weid- ("to see, to know")
  → Proto-Germanic *wīsaz ("wise, knowing, having seen")
    → Old English wīsdōm ("wisdom") — wīs (wise) + dōm (state/judgment)
      → Middle English wisdom → Modern English "wisdom"

Cognates: wise, wit, wizard, vision, video (Latin vidēre, "to see")
Key insight: wisdom = "having seen" — the knowledge gained through experience and sight.

Greek:

  → Used for both human wisdom and divine wisdom (1 Cor 1:24 — Christ is God's sophia)
  → φρόνησις (phronēsis, G5428) — practical wisdom, prudence, good judgment
  → σοφός (sophos, G4680) — wise; philosopher = "lover of wisdom" (philo-sophia)
