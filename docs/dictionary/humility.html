Humility
/hjuːˈmɪl.ɪ.ti/
noun
Latin humilitas (lowness, smallness, insignificance); from humilis (low, on the ground), from humus (earth, ground). To be humble is to be close to the ground — a creature among creatures, not self-elevated. Hebrew: anavah (עֲנָוָה) — meekness, gentleness, lowliness of spirit.

📖 Biblical Definition

Biblical humility is accurate self-knowledge in light of God's knowledge — not self-deprecation, but truthful orientation. It begins with recognizing that we are creatures before our Creator, sinners before our Savior, recipients before our Provider. Humility is not weakness; it is the prerequisite for all other virtues. "God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble" (James 4:6). The supreme model of humility is Christ, who "emptied Himself… taking the form of a servant" and "humbled Himself by becoming obedient to the point of death" (Phil. 2:7–8). Humility opens the hand to receive grace; pride clenches the fist against it. C.S. Lewis noted: "Humility is not thinking less of yourself; it is thinking of yourself less."

Philippians 2:3–8 — "Do nothing from selfish ambition or conceit, but in humility count others more significant than yourselves… Have this mind among yourselves, which is yours in Christ Jesus, who… emptied Himself, taking the form of a servant."

James 4:6 — "God opposes the proud but gives grace to the humble."

Micah 6:8 — "He has told you, O man, what is good; and what does the LORD require of you but to do justice, and to love kindness, and to walk humbly with your God?"

Matthew 23:12 — "Whoever exalts himself will be humbled, and whoever humbles himself will be exalted."

1 Peter 5:6 — "Humble yourselves, therefore, under the mighty hand of God so that at the proper time He may exalt you."

🔗 Greek & Hebrew Roots

H6038 — anavah (עֲנָוָה): humility, meekness, gentleness; from H6035 anav (poor, afflicted, humble). The humble person is not crushed but has chosen not to self-elevate.

G5012 — tapeinophrosynē (ταπεινοφροσύνη): humility, lowliness of mind; from tapeinos (low) + phrēn (mind). A posture of the mind, not merely external behavior.

Usage

"The humble man does not shrink from the room — he refuses to be the center of it."

"Pride builds walls; humility opens doors."

"Every advance in sanctification is an advance in humility — the closer you get to the Light, the more clearly you see the darkness that remains."

Secular culture both mocks and performs humility. "Humblebragging" — the art of self-promotion through false modesty — has become its own genre. Simultaneously, therapeutic culture rejects genuine humility as psychologically unhealthy, conflating it with low self-esteem or codependence. "You need to love yourself first" is the counter-gospel. The therapeutic tradition is right that self-hatred is not virtuous; but it mistakes biblical humility — grounded in accurate knowledge of who God is and who we are — for the pathological self-loathing that comes from distorted identity. Humility before God is the source of dignity before men, not its enemy.
