Authority
/ɔːˈθɒr.ɪ.ti/
noun
Middle English autorite, from Old French autorité, from Latin auctoritas — influence, command, power; from auctor meaning originator, promoter. Rooted in augere: to increase, to originate.

📖 Biblical Definition

Delegated power granted by God for righteous governance and servant leadership. All authority belongs to God (Romans 13:1) and is delegated through structures He ordains: government, church, family, and workplace. Authority is not self-generated — it is received, accountable, and purposeful. Those in authority will give account to God for how they wielded it. Biblical authority always serves those under it rather than exploiting them.

📜 Webster 1828 Definition

AUTHOR'ITY, n. Legal power, or a right to command or to act; power; rule; dominion. Authority in individuals, is derived from God, or from law, from the consent of the governed, or from custom and usage. Legitimate authority is that which has legal sanction and divine approval.

⚠️ Modern Corruption

Post-modern culture treats authority as inherently suspect — something to be deconstructed, resisted, and dismantled. All authority structures are reframed as power systems maintaining oppression (patriarchy, white supremacy, etc.). The result is the dissolution of legitimate authority — in government, church, and family — leading not to liberation but to chaos, because humans were designed to live under right authority, not without it.

📖 Key Scripture
