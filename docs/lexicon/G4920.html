G4920 · Greek · New Testament
συνίημι
Suniemi
Verb
To Understand / To Comprehend / To Perceive

Definition

The Greek verb suniemi (συνίημι) means to understand, perceive, or put together in one's mind — to bring things together into comprehension. It appears about 26 times in the NT, concentrated in the Gospels.

Usage & Theological Significance

Suniemi is the word used most often in the Gospels to describe the disciples' varying levels of comprehension. In Matthew 13:13–23 (the Parable of the Sower), those who understand (suniemi) the word are the ones in whom it bears fruit. Jesus said He spoke in parables because 'seeing they do not see, and hearing they do not hear, nor do they understand' — quoting Isaiah 6:9–10. Understanding is not merely intellectual — it is spiritual and requires the opening of the heart. After the resurrection, 'he opened their minds so they could understand (sunienai) the Scriptures' (Luke 24:45). Comprehension is always a gift from God.

Key Bible Verses

Matthew 13:23 But the seed falling on good soil refers to someone who hears the word and understands (sunion) it.
Luke 24:45 Then he opened their minds so they could understand (sunienai) the Scriptures.
Romans 3:11 There is no one who understands (sunioon); there is no one who seeks God.
Matthew 15:10 Jesus called the crowd to him and said, 'Listen and understand (suniete).
Ephesians 5:17 Therefore do not be foolish, but understand (suniente) what the Lord's will is.
