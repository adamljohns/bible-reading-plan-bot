G3982 · Greek · New Testament
πείθω
peithō
Verb
To persuade, trust, obey, have confidence

Definition

A Greek verb meaning to persuade, convince, win over, trust, have confidence in, obey, rely upon. In the active it means to persuade or convince. In the perfect passive (pepoitha) it means 'I am persuaded, I have confidence, I trust.' The perfect tense — a settled, ongoing state of persuasion — is theologically significant.

Usage & Theological Significance

The perfect tense of peithō produces some of the New Testament's most powerful declarations of assurance. Paul writes, 'I am persuaded (pepoitha) that neither death nor life... shall be able to separate us from the love of God' (Romans 8:38) — this is not mere opinion but settled, immovable conviction. 'I am persuaded that he is able to guard what I have entrusted to him' (2 Timothy 1:12) — Paul's confidence rests in God's character, not his own. The obedience meaning in Hebrews 13:17 ('Obey your leaders') literally means 'be persuaded by' — suggesting that true spiritual authority persuades rather than coerces. Biblical faith is being persuaded by God and staying persuaded.

Key Bible Verses

Romans 8:38 For I am persuaded that neither death nor life, nor angels nor rulers, shall be able to separate us from the love of God.
2 Timothy 1:12 For I know whom I have believed, and I am persuaded that he is able to guard until that day what has been entrusted to me.
Philippians 1:6 And I am sure (persuaded) of this, that he who began a good work in you will bring it to completion.
Hebrews 13:17 Obey (be persuaded by) your leaders and submit to them, for they are keeping watch over your souls.
Acts 28:24 And some were convinced by what he said, but others disbelieved.
