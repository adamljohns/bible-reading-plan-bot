H6213 · Hebrew · Old Testament
עָשָׂה
asah
Verb
to do, to make

Definition

One of the most common verbs, about 2,630 times. Means to do, make, produce, or accomplish. While bara is reserved for divine creation and yatsar for shaping, asah is general verb of making — used for both God and humans.

Usage & Theological Significance

God asah the heavens and earth (Gen 2:4), the luminaries (1:16), all living things. Humans asah garments, tools, sacrifices, covenants. Ethical imperative is to asah justice and righteousness (Gen 18:19, Mic 6:8). 'To hear and to do' — shama and asah — summarizes faithful covenant response.

Key Bible Verses

Genesis 1:16 God made the two great lights.
Micah 6:8 To do justice, love kindness, walk humbly.
Psalm 118:24 This is the day the LORD has made.
Isaiah 55:11 It shall accomplish that which I purpose.
Genesis 18:19 They shall keep the way of the LORD by doing righteousness.

Word Study

Carries sense of practical accomplishment. God's word does not return void but accomplishes (asah) its purpose (Isa 55:11). Phrase asah chesed ('do lovingkindness') shows chesed is not passive sentiment but active deed. 'Doers of the word' is concept rooted in this verb.

Related Words

H1254 — bara (to create) H3335 — yatsar (to form) H8104 — shamar (to keep)
