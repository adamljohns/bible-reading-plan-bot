G2127 · Greek · New Testament
εὐλογέω
eulogeō
Verb
To bless, speak well of, praise

Definition

A Greek verb meaning to bless, speak well of, praise, give thanks, consecrate. From eu (well, good) and logos (word) — literally 'to speak a good word.' It is used of God blessing people, people blessing God (praising), Jesus blessing bread, and believers blessing one another. The cognate eulogia gives us English 'eulogy.'

Usage & Theological Significance

The theology of eulogeō flows in three directions: God blesses people (bestowing favor), people bless God (returning praise), and people bless others (speaking good over them). In Ephesians 1:3, Paul says God 'has blessed us in Christ with every spiritual blessing' — this is a completed, comprehensive blessing. Jesus blessed the loaves before multiplication (Mark 6:41) — connecting blessing with abundance. Peter declares that God sent Christ 'to bless you by turning every one of you from your wickedness' (Acts 3:26) — God's ultimate blessing is transformation, not just provision. The command to 'bless those who persecute you' (Romans 12:14) is revolutionary: speak good words over those who speak evil against you.

Key Bible Verses

Ephesians 1:3 Blessed be the God and Father of our Lord Jesus Christ, who has blessed us in Christ with every spiritual blessing.
Mark 6:41 And taking the five loaves and the two fish, he looked up to heaven and said a blessing and broke the loaves.
Romans 12:14 Bless those who persecute you; bless and do not curse them.
Luke 24:50 Then he led them out as far as Bethany, and lifting up his hands he blessed them.
Acts 3:26 God, having raised up his servant, sent him to you first, to bless you by turning every one of you from your wickedness.
