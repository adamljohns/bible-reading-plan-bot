H6310 · Hebrew · Old Testament
פֶּה
Peh
Noun, masculine
Mouth, speech, opening

Definition

Peh means mouth, speech, edge, or opening. It appears about 500 times. It refers to the physical mouth, the act of speaking, the edge of a sword, and the opening of a well or pit.

Usage & Theological Significance

The mouth is the instrument of both blessing and cursing, truth and deception. God's mouth speaks creation into being and utters His law. 'Man shall not live by bread alone but by every word that comes from the mouth of God' (Deuteronomy 8:3) establishes God's speech as humanity's true sustenance.

Key Bible Verses

Deuteronomy 8:3 Every word that comes from the mouth of the LORD.
Psalm 19:14 Let the words of my mouth be acceptable.
Proverbs 18:21 The tongue has the power of life and death.
Joshua 1:8 Keep this Book of the Law always on your lips.
Isaiah 55:11 So is my word that goes out from my mouth.
