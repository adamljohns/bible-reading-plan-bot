G5293 · Greek · New Testament
ὑποτάσσω
hypotassō
Verb
To submit, subject, put in order under

Definition

A Greek verb meaning to place under, subordinate, subject, put in subjection, submit oneself. A military term (hypo = under, tassō = to arrange/order) — to arrange under a commander. In the middle voice, it means 'to voluntarily place oneself under' — a deliberate, chosen submission rather than forced subjugation.

Usage & Theological Significance

The theology of hypotassō is deeply nuanced and often misunderstood. Christ subjected Himself to the Father's will (Luke 2:51; 1 Corinthians 15:28) — His submission was voluntary, not indicating inferiority. Believers are called to mutual submission: 'submitting to one another out of reverence for Christ' (Ephesians 5:21) — this is the heading that governs all the household codes that follow. The middle voice is crucial: biblical submission is never forced subjugation — it is a voluntary, Spirit-empowered decision to place oneself under. Even creation itself 'was subjected to futility' but 'in hope' (Romans 8:20) — God's subjection of creation to decay was temporary, aimed at redemption. Submission in Scripture is not about worth or value but about order, trust, and voluntary love.

Key Bible Verses

Ephesians 5:21 Submitting to one another out of reverence for Christ.
Romans 8:20 For the creation was subjected to futility, not willingly, but because of him who subjected it, in hope.
1 Corinthians 15:28 When all things are subjected to him, then the Son himself will also be subjected to him who put all things in subjection under him.
James 4:7 Submit yourselves therefore to God. Resist the devil, and he will flee from you.
1 Peter 2:13 Be subject for the Lord's sake to every human institution.
