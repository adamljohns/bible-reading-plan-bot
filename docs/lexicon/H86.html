H86 · Hebrew · Old Testament
אֲדוֹן
Adon
Noun, masculine
Lord, master, owner

Definition

The Hebrew noun adon means lord, master, or sovereign. It is used over 300 times in the Old Testament to describe human masters, owners, rulers, and most importantly, God Himself. The plural form Adonai (my lords) became the standard Jewish substitution for the divine name YHWH.

Usage & Theological Significance

In everyday usage, adon referred to a husband, master, or king. But its greatest significance lies in its application to God. Adonai — the plural of majesty with first-person suffix — became the reverent title Jews used when reading the sacred name YHWH aloud. It expresses God's absolute sovereignty, ownership of creation, and authority over all things. Psalm 110:1 — 'The LORD says to my Lord: Sit at my right hand' — was the most quoted OT verse in the New Testament, applied to Jesus as the divine Adon.

Key Bible Verses

Psalm 110:1 The LORD says to my Lord: 'Sit at my right hand until I make your enemies a footstool for your feet.'
Genesis 18:3 He said, 'If I have found favor in your eyes, my Lord, do not pass your servant by.'
Nehemiah 8:10 Then Nehemiah the governor, Ezra the priest and teacher of the Law... said to all the people: 'This day is holy to our Lord. Do not grieve, for the joy of the LORD is your strength.'
Malachi 1:6 A son honors his father, and a slave his master (adon). If I am a father, where is the honor due me? If I am a master, where is the respect due me?
Psalm 8:1 LORD, our Lord, how majestic is your name in all the earth!
