H5750 · Hebrew · Old Testament
עוֹד
od
Adverb / Noun
still, yet, again, more, while, besides

Definition

Od is a Hebrew particle expressing continuation, repetition, or addition. It can mean "still" (ongoing action), "yet" (anticipating a future state), "again" (repetition), or "more/besides" (addition). It appears about 490 times.

As an adverb attached to a verb, it can extend an action into the present or future. Alone, it can be nominal ("there is yet more"). Its range makes it context-sensitive — but all senses cluster around the idea of continuation beyond what has already occurred.

Usage & Theological Significance

One of the most beloved uses of od is in Psalm 23:6: "Surely goodness and mercy shall follow me all the days of my life, and I shall dwell in the house of the LORD forever (le-orek yamim / od, in the sense of continuance)." The psalm ends with an affirmation of perpetual divine companionship.

The prophetic "yet" (od) signals hope beyond judgment. Isaiah 11:11 — "It will happen again (od) in that day that the Lord will set his hand the second time to recover the remnant." There is an "od" horizon — what God did before, He will do again, and more.

Ruth 1:11 uses od poignantly: "Are there yet (od) more sons in my womb…?" Naomi's desolation is articulated as the absence of any "more." The gospel is ultimately God providing what remains (od) when human resources run dry.

Key Bible Verses

Genesis 29:9 While Jacob was still (od) talking with them, Rachel came with her father's sheep.
Jeremiah 31:4 I will build you up again (od), and you, Virgin Israel, will be rebuilt.
Zechariah 8:4 Again (od) old men and old women will sit in the streets of Jerusalem, each of them staff in hand.
