H5892 · Hebrew · Old Testament
עִיר
Ir
Noun, masculine/feminine
City / Town

Definition

The Hebrew word ir (also spelled ayin-yod-resh) means a city or town — an enclosed, inhabited settlement. It is one of the most common nouns in the Hebrew Bible, appearing over 1,000 times. Cities were places of protection (walled), commerce, worship (temples and gates), justice (the elders sat at the gate), and community. From the first city Cain built (Genesis 4:17) to the New Jerusalem descending from heaven (Revelation 21), the city is a central organizing image of Scripture.

Usage & Theological Significance

The biblical theology of the city moves from Babel (human pride) to Babylon (empire and idolatry) to Jerusalem (the city of God) to the New Jerusalem (the eternal dwelling). God chose Jerusalem as His city: I have chosen Jerusalem for my Name to be there (2 Chronicles 6:6). The Psalms celebrate Zion as the City of God (Psalm 46:4, 48:1–2). Jesus wept over Jerusalem (Luke 19:41) and was crucified outside its walls. Hebrews declares that believers are citizens of the heavenly city (Hebrews 12:22), and Revelation presents the consummated City as the ultimate home of God's people.
