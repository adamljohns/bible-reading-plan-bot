G3056 · Greek · New Testament
λόγος
logos
Noun, masculine
Word, message, reason, the Word

Definition

One of the richest words in the Greek language: a word, speech, message, account, reason, or matter. It encompasses everything from a single uttered word to a complete discourse, from rational thought to divine revelation. In the prologue of John's Gospel, Logos becomes a christological title of supreme importance — the pre-existent divine Word through whom all things were made.

Usage & Theological Significance

John 1:1 — 'In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God' — is the most theologically dense sentence in the NT. The Logos is not merely God's speech but His self-expression — the one through whom God creates, reveals, and redeems. 'The Word became flesh and dwelt among us' (John 1:14) is the incarnation in seven words. Hebrews 4:12 describes the logos of God as 'living and active, sharper than any two-edged sword' — a word that judges and penetrates. In Revelation, Christ rides forth as a warrior-king whose name is 'The Word of God' (Rev 19:13). The gospel itself is called 'the word' (Mark 4:14-20; Acts 6:7), and its spread is described as 'the word of God grew and multiplied' (Acts 12:24). In logos, speech and being, revelation and reality, are perfectly united.

Key Bible Verses

John 1:1 In the beginning was the Word, and the Word was with God, and the Word was God.
John 1:14 And the Word was made flesh, and dwelt among us, and we beheld his glory.
Hebrews 4:12 For the word of God is quick, and powerful, and sharper than any twoedged sword.
Revelation 19:13 And he was clothed with a vesture dipped in blood: and his name is called The Word of God.
Romans 10:17 So then faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God.
