G386 · Greek · New Testament
ἀνάστασις
Anastasis
Noun, feminine
Resurrection

Definition

Anastasis means resurrection or rising up. It appears about 40 times. It describes Jesus' resurrection, the general resurrection of the dead, and spiritual resurrection to new life.

Usage & Theological Significance

Christ's resurrection (anastasis) is the cornerstone of Christian faith (1 Corinthians 15). It validates His claims, defeats death, and guarantees believers' future resurrection. Resurrection is both a past event (Christ rose) and a future hope (we will rise). Spiritual resurrection happens now; bodily resurrection awaits the last day.

Key Bible Verses

1 Corinthians 15:14 If Christ has not been raised, our preaching is useless.
John 11:25 I am the resurrection and the life.
Acts 17:18 He was preaching the good news about Jesus and the resurrection.
Romans 6:5 If we have been united with him in a death like his, we will certainly be united with him in a resurrection like his.
Philippians 3:10 I want to know Christ — yes, to know the power of his resurrection.
