H3722 · Hebrew · Old Testament
כָּפַר
kaphar
Verb
atone, make atonement, cover, ransom

Definition

The central sacrificial verb of the OT — to cover, wipe away, or make amends for sin. The noun kopher (ransom price) and the great Day of Atonement (Yom Kippur) come from this root. The precise mechanism — whether 'cover' or 'wipe away' — is debated, but the effect is clear: guilt is dealt with, access to God is restored.

Usage & Theological Significance

Kaphar is the theological heart of Leviticus. The whole sacrificial system — blood, altar, priest, Day of Atonement — exists to make atonement for Israel's sins before a holy God. The NT interprets Christ's death through this lens: he is the ultimate atoning sacrifice, the one who does what all the Levitical offerings pointed toward but could never fully accomplish (Hebrews 10).

Key Bible Verses

Leviticus 16:30 For on that day shall the priest make an atonement for you, to cleanse you, that ye may be clean.
Psalm 79:9 Help us, O God of our salvation... and purge away our sins, for thy name's sake.
Isaiah 53:10 When thou shalt make his soul an offering for sin, he shall see his seed.
