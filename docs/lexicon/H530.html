H530 · Hebrew · Old Testament
אֱמוּנָה
ʾĕmûnâ
Noun, feminine
Faithfulness, steadfastness, fidelity

Definition

Derived from the root ʾāman (H539, to confirm, support, be faithful), this noun denotes firmness, reliability, and trustworthiness. It describes both God's unwavering covenant loyalty and the human response of steadfast trust. Unlike the English word 'faith' (which can sound passive), ʾĕmûnâ carries the force of active, enduring reliability — like a pillar that does not move.

Usage & Theological Significance

Habakkuk 2:4 — 'the just shall live by his faithfulness (ʾĕmûnâ)' — became one of the most quoted OT texts in the NT (Rom 1:17; Gal 3:11; Heb 10:38), forming the backbone of Paul's theology of justification. The word predominantly describes God's character: His faithfulness endures to all generations (Ps 100:5), reaches to the clouds (Ps 36:5), and is the girdle of the Messiah's waist (Isa 11:5). When applied to humans, it denotes not mere belief but lived fidelity — the righteous person whose life is built on the bedrock of God's reliability. Moses' hands held up in ʾĕmûnâ (Exod 17:12) while Joshua fought Amalek is a vivid image of sustained, embodied faithfulness.

Key Bible Verses

Habakkuk 2:4 Behold, his soul which is lifted up is not upright in him: but the just shall live by his faith.
Psalm 36:5 Thy mercy, O LORD, is in the heavens; and thy faithfulness reacheth unto the clouds.
Psalm 100:5 For the LORD is good; his mercy is everlasting; and his truth endureth to all generations.
Lamentations 3:23 They are new every morning: great is thy faithfulness.
Isaiah 11:5 And righteousness shall be the girdle of his loins, and faithfulness the girdle of his reins.
