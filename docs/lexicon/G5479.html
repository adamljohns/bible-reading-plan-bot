G5479 · Greek · New Testament
χαρά
chara
Noun, feminine
joy, gladness, delight

Definition

Joy, gladness, delight — the deep-seated sense of wellbeing that comes from God's presence and promises. Chara is related to charis (grace) — joy is the natural response to grace. It is not circumstantial happiness but a fruit of the Spirit that persists through suffering.

Usage & Theological Significance

The angels announced 'great joy' at Christ's birth (Luke 2:10). Jesus promised, 'Your joy no one will take from you' (John 16:22). Paul commands, 'Rejoice in the Lord always' (Philippians 4:4) — from prison! Joy is listed as the second fruit of the Spirit (Galatians 5:22). James says to 'count it all joy' when facing trials (1:2). Christian joy is paradoxical — deepest in suffering because it rests not on circumstances but on God.

Key Bible Verses

John 16:22 Your joy no one will take from you.
Philippians 4:4 Rejoice in the Lord always. I will say it again: Rejoice!
Galatians 5:22 The fruit of the Spirit is love, joy, peace.
James 1:2 Count it all joy when you meet trials of various kinds.
Luke 2:10 I bring you good news of great joy that will be for all the people.
