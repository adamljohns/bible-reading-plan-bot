H2421 · Hebrew · Old Testament
חָיָה
chāyâ
Verb
To live, be alive, revive

Definition

The verbal root of chay (H2416, living, alive) and chayyîm (life). It encompasses being alive, staying alive, being restored to life (revived), and causing to live (preserving life). In the Piel stem, it means to let live, keep alive, or restore. Related to Chavvâ (Eve), the 'mother of all living.'

Usage & Theological Significance

Life belongs to God — He alone gives it and sustains it. The name Chavvâ (Eve) from this root declares that life is God's gift even after the fall (Gen 3:20). God's repeated self-description as 'the living God' (ʾĕlōhîm chayyîm) distinguishes Him from dead idols (Josh 3:10; Ps 42:2). The great promise of revival — 'I will put my Spirit in you, and you shall live' (Ezek 37:14) — comes through this verb in the valley of dry bones vision, the OT's most powerful image of resurrection. Habakkuk 2:4 declares 'the just shall live (yichyeh) by his faithfulness,' linking chāyâ to the life of faith. Deuteronomy 30:19 frames the covenant choice starkly: 'I have set before thee life and death… choose life.'

Key Bible Verses

Ezekiel 37:5 Thus saith the Lord GOD unto these bones; Behold, I will cause breath to enter into you, and ye shall live.
Deuteronomy 30:19 I call heaven and earth to record this day against you, that I have set before you life and death… therefore choose life.
Psalm 119:25 My soul cleaveth unto the dust: quicken thou me according to thy word.
Habakkuk 2:4 Behold, his soul which is lifted up is not upright in him: but the just shall live by his faith.
Psalm 42:2 My soul thirsteth for God, for the living God: when shall I come and appear before God?
