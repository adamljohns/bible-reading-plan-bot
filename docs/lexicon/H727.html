H727 · Hebrew · Old Testament
אָרוֹן
ʾārôn
Noun, masculine
ark, chest, coffin

Definition

The Hebrew ʾārôn refers to any box or chest, but it reaches its fullest theological significance as the Ark of the Covenant (ʾărôn habberît) — the gold-overlaid acacia-wood chest that housed the two tablets of the Law, Aaron's rod, and a jar of manna. Constructed in Exodus 25 according to God's precise instructions, it was kept in the Holy of Holies and served as the footstool of the invisible, enthroned God. The ark symbolized God's presence dwelling with his people. The word also appears for a simple chest (2 Kings 12:9) and for Joseph's coffin (Genesis 50:26).

Usage & Theological Significance

ʾĀrôn occupied the innermost chamber of the Tabernacle and Temple — the Most Holy Place — accessible only to the high priest on Yom Kippur. It was the central focal point of Israel's worship system, representing the meeting place between the holy God and his covenant people. The mercy seat (kappōret) above it became the site of atonement. Its capture by the Philistines (1 Samuel 4–6) was treated as a national catastrophe tantamount to God's departure. In Revelation 11:19, the heavenly counterpart of the ark appears, signaling ultimate covenant fulfillment.

Key Bible Verses

Exodus 25:10-11 Have them make an ark [ʾārôn] of acacia wood — two and a half cubits long, a cubit and a half wide, and a cubit and a half high. Overlay it with pure gold.
Numbers 10:35 Whenever the ark [ʾārôn] set out, Moses said, 'Rise up, LORD! May your enemies be scattered.'
Joshua 3:11 The ark of the covenant [ʾărôn habberît] of the Lord of all the earth will go into the Jordan ahead of you.
1 Samuel 4:11 The ark of God [ʾărôn ʾĕlōhîm] was captured, and Hophni and Phinehas, the two sons of Eli, died.
1 Kings 8:9 There was nothing in the ark [ʾārôn] except the two stone tablets that Moses had placed in it at Horeb.
