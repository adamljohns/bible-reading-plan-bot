H1 · Hebrew · Old Testament
אָב
ab
Noun, masculine
father, ancestor, originator

Definition

The primary Hebrew word for father, appearing over 1,200 times. Ab denotes not only a biological father but also a grandfather, ancestor, founder of a household, or originator. God Himself is called Ab (Isaiah 63:16).

Usage & Theological Significance

In ancient Israelite culture, the father held supreme authority in the household. The concept extends to tribal patriarchs. When God is called Father, it conveys His covenantal care, discipline, and tenderness. Jesus revolutionized this by inviting believers to call God Abba.

Key Bible Verses

Genesis 2:24 Therefore a man shall leave his father and mother.
Exodus 20:12 Honor your father and mother.
Psalm 68:5 A father of the fatherless.
Proverbs 4:1 Hear the instruction of a father.
Isaiah 63:16 You, O LORD, are our Father.

Word Study

The root appears in many compound names: Abraham ('father of many'), Abimelech ('my father is king'). The word carries connotations of authority, provision, protection, and instruction. The plural aboth refers to ancestors.
