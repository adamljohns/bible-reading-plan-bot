H1580 · Hebrew · Old Testament
גָּמַל
gamal
Verb
deal with, repay, reward, ripen, wean

Definition

A verb with a surprising range: to deal bountifully, to repay (good or evil), to ripen (of fruit), and to wean (a child). The common thread is bringing something to completion or fullness. God gamals — he deals with people according to what they deserve, and he deals bountifully with those who trust him.

Usage & Theological Significance

The Psalmist's refrain 'the LORD has dealt bountifully with me' (Ps 13:6; 116:7; 119:17) uses gamal to describe God's generous treatment. Psalm 103:10 is the flip-side: 'He has not dealt with us according to our sins' — the expected gamal (just repayment) was suspended by mercy. Proverbs 31:12 uses it for the virtuous wife: 'she does him good (gamal) and not harm all the days of her life.'

Key Bible Verses

Psalm 116:7 Return unto thy rest, O my soul; for the LORD hath dealt bountifully with thee.
Psalm 103:10 He hath not dealt with us after our sins; nor rewarded us according to our iniquities.
Proverbs 31:12 She will do him good and not evil all the days of her life.
