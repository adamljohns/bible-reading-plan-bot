H6697 · Hebrew · Old Testament
צוּר
tsur
Noun (masculine)
rock, cliff, boulder; God as Rock (divine title)

Definition

Tsur (H6697) means rock, cliff, boulder — typically a large, immovable rock formation rather than a small stone (eben). It appears approximately 80 times in the Old Testament, frequently as a divine title: 'The Rock' becomes one of the most exalted names for God in Hebrew Scripture, particularly in the Song of Moses (Deuteronomy 32) and the Psalms. A related verb tsur (H6696) means 'to confine, besiege' — a different root.

Usage & Theological Significance

Tsur as a divine title is one of the most evocative theological metaphors in the Hebrew Bible. A rock embodies stability, permanence, and impenetrability — the perfect image of a God who does not change, cannot be moved, and provides absolute security. Moses' song begins: 'Ascribe greatness to our God! The Rock, his work is perfect' (Deuteronomy 32:3-4). The Psalms are saturated with this imagery: 'The LORD is my rock and my fortress' (Psalm 18:2); 'Who is a rock, except our God?' (Psalm 18:31).

Paul identifies the Rock that provided water in the wilderness as Christ: 'They drank from the spiritual Rock that followed them, and the Rock was Christ' (1 Corinthians 10:4). The imagery culminates in Peter's confession and Jesus' response: upon this rock (Greek petra) I will build my church (Matthew 16:18). The eternal Rock is both refuge and foundation — the source of life and the ground of the church's existence.
