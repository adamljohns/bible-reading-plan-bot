H2428 · Hebrew · Old Testament
חַיִל
chayil
Noun, masculine
strength, valor, wealth

Definition

Wide semantic range, about 245 times. Means strength, capability, valor, wealth, military force. Famous eshet chayil ('woman of valor,' Prov 31:10) describes outstanding capability/character.

Usage & Theological Significance

God's deliverance described in chayil language. Ruth called eshet chayil (3:11), Boaz ish gibbor chayil (2:1). Zechariah: 'Not by might [chayil], but by my Spirit' (4:6) — redefines strength.

Key Bible Verses

Proverbs 31:10 An excellent woman [eshet chayil] who can find?
Zechariah 4:6 Not by might [chayil], but by my Spirit.
Deuteronomy 8:18 He gives you power to get wealth.
Ruth 3:11 You are a woman of valor.
Psalm 33:16 The king is not saved by his great army.

Word Study

Root may relate to chay (life), suggesting vital energy. Contexts: military force (Ex 14:4), personal wealth (Deut 8:18), physical strength (1 Sam 2:4), moral excellence (Prov 31:10).

Related Words

H1368 — gibbor (mighty one) H3581 — koach (strength) H2416 — chay (living)
