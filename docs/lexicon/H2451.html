H2451 · Hebrew · Old Testament
חׇכְמָה
Chokmah
Noun, feminine
Wisdom, skill, insight

Definition

Chokmah means wisdom, skill, or shrewd insight. It appears about 150 times. It encompasses practical skill, moral discernment, intellectual understanding, and — supremely — the fear of the LORD as the beginning of all true wisdom.

Usage & Theological Significance

Biblical wisdom is not mere intelligence but the skill of living rightly before God. Chokmah is personified in Proverbs 8 as present with God at creation, leading the church to see Christ as the embodiment of divine wisdom (1 Corinthians 1:24). All reality is morally ordered by God.

Key Bible Verses

Proverbs 9:10 The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom.
Proverbs 8:22 The LORD brought me forth as the first of his works.
1 Kings 3:12 I will give you a wise and discerning heart.
Proverbs 4:7 Get wisdom. Though it cost all you have, get understanding.
Job 28:28 The fear of the Lord — that is wisdom.
