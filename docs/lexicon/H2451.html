H2451 · Hebrew · Old Testament
חָכְמָה
chokmah
Noun, feminine
wisdom, skill

Definition

Hebrew word for wisdom, about 150 times. Far more than intellectual knowledge — practical skill for living well, moral discernment, ultimately the fear of the LORD. In Prov 8, Wisdom personified, present at creation.

Usage & Theological Significance

Solomon asked for chokmah (1 Kgs 3:9). Wisdom tradition explores from multiple angles. Personification in Prov 8 — present before creation, delighting in mankind — anticipates Logos theology of John 1.

Key Bible Verses

Proverbs 9:10 The fear of the LORD is the beginning of wisdom.
Proverbs 8:22 The LORD possessed me [Wisdom] at the beginning.
1 Kings 3:12 I give you a wise and discerning mind.
Proverbs 4:7 Wisdom is the principal thing.
Job 28:28 Behold, the fear of the Lord, that is wisdom.

Word Study

Root produces chakam (wise person), chokmah (wisdom), chokmoth (teachings). Applies to artisans (skill in craftsmanship), rulers (wisdom to govern), ordinary people (skill in living). True chokmah begins and ends with fear of the LORD.

Related Words
