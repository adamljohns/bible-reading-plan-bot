H5828 · Hebrew · Old Testament
עֵזֶר
Ezer
Noun, masculine
Help, helper

Definition

The Hebrew noun ezer means help or helper — one who provides aid, support, or assistance in time of need. It occurs 21 times in the Old Testament and is applied both to human helpers and to God Himself as the supreme helper. Most famously, it describes Eve as Adam's 'helper' in Genesis 2:18.

Usage & Theological Significance

The theological weight of ezer is often missed when applied to Eve. Of the 21 uses of the word, 16 describe God as Israel's helper (Psalms 121:2; 124:8; Deuteronomy 33:29). Ezer is the word used for a military ally, a rescuer, a vital support without whom a mission fails. Calling Eve Adam's ezer is therefore not diminishing — it is elevating. She is a necessary, powerful co-laborer. Furthermore, when Jesus promised 'another Helper' (Greek: paraklētos, John 14:16), the concept reaches fulfillment: God's Spirit as the ultimate ezer, dwelling within believers as the source of strength, guidance, and divine help in all circumstances.

Key Bible Verses

Genesis 2:18 The LORD God said, 'It is not good for the man to be alone. I will make a helper suitable for him.'
Psalm 121:2 My help comes from the LORD, the Maker of heaven and earth.
Deuteronomy 33:29 Blessed are you, Israel! Who is like you, a people saved by the LORD? He is your shield and helper and your glorious sword.
Psalm 46:1 God is our refuge and strength, an ever-present help in trouble.
Hosea 13:9 You are destroyed, Israel, because you are against me, against your helper.
