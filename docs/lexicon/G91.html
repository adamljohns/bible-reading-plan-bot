G91 · Greek · New Testament
ἀδικέω
Adikeō
Verb
To Do Wrong / Injure / Act Unjustly

Definition

The Greek verb adikeō comes from adikos (G94, unjust) = a- (not) + dikaios (G1342, righteous/just). It means to do wrong, act unjustly, harm, or injure. It can refer to wrongs against other people (Colossians 3:25), wrongs against God (Revelation 22:11), and the cosmic injustice of sin. The noun form adikia (G93) is often translated 'unrighteousness' or 'iniquity.'

Usage & Theological Significance

Adikeō captures the active, relational dimension of sin — sin is not merely a state of impurity but a wrongful action against God and neighbor. Paul teaches that the wages of sin is death precisely because sin is cosmic injustice — it wrongs the Creator. The Gospel addresses this directly: Christ, who never did wrong (1 Peter 2:22), took the penalty for our wrongdoing (2 Corinthians 5:21). In the final judgment, Let the one who does wrong continue to do wrong; let the vile person continue to be vile (Revelation 22:11) — a sobering call to repentance while there is still time.

Key Bible Verses

Colossians 3:25 Anyone who does wrong will be repaid for their wrong, and there is no favoritism.
Romans 9:14 What then shall we say? Is God unjust? Not at all!
Luke 10:19 I have given you authority to trample on snakes and scorpions and to overcome all the power of the enemy; nothing will harm you.
Revelation 22:11 Let the one who does wrong continue to do wrong; let the vile person continue to be vile; let the one who does right continue to do right.
Galatians 4:12 Become like me, for I became like you. You did me no wrong.
