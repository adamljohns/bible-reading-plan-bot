G3772 · Greek · New Testament
οὐρανός
ouranos
Noun, masculine
heaven, sky, the heavens

Definition

Heaven, the sky, the heavens — referring to the physical sky, the celestial realm, or God's dwelling place. Ouranos can mean the atmosphere, the starry heavens, or the spiritual realm where God reigns. 'The kingdom of heaven' in Matthew is the same as 'the kingdom of God' in Mark and Luke.

Usage & Theological Significance

Heaven is not merely a future destination but a present reality that shapes Christian life. The Lord's Prayer envisions heaven's will done on earth. Paul says believers' citizenship is in heaven (Philippians 3:20). Christ ascended to heaven and sits at God's right hand. Revelation's new heaven and new earth (21:1) promise the final union of heavenly and earthly realms. Heaven is wherever God reigns fully.

Key Bible Verses

Matthew 6:10 Your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven.
Philippians 3:20 Our citizenship is in heaven.
Revelation 21:1 I saw a new heaven and a new earth.
Matthew 6:9 Our Father in heaven, hallowed be your name.
Acts 1:11 This same Jesus will come back in the same way you have seen him go into heaven.
