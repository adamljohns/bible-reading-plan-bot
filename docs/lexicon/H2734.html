H2734 · Hebrew · Old Testament
חָרָה
charah
Verb
burn with anger, be hot, be incensed

Definition

The verb of fiery anger — literally, to become hot or to burn. Used of human anger and of divine wrath, it captures the visceral, heat-metaphor that biblical Hebrew uses for rage. God's charah is not capricious but always righteous — the burning response of holiness to evil.

Usage & Theological Significance

Divine anger in the OT is a crucial theological category: God's wrath is not a divine temper-tantrum but the holy fire of his justice against sin and covenant-breaking. Psalms like 2, 7, and 89 grapple with God's burning anger. The danger is presuming on God's patience — the same God whose compassions are new every morning is the God whose anger 'burns like fire' (Jer 4:4) against persistent rebellion.

Key Bible Verses

Psalm 2:12 Kiss the Son, lest he be angry, and ye perish from the way, when his wrath is kindled but a little.
Exodus 4:14 And the anger of the LORD was kindled against Moses.
Genesis 4:5 And Cain was very wroth, and his countenance fell.
